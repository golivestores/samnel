HSA, GMP, FSSC, SGS: what Samnel's certifications actually mean

Four quality frameworks, one transparent product

Every Samnel product passes through four independent quality frameworks before it reaches a consumer. Each one guarantees something specific — and together they tell a story about what Samnel is willing to be audited on. Here is what each framework actually means.

HSA (Singapore Health Sciences Authority) is Singapore’s regulatory authority for therapeutic products, cosmetics, and health supplements. HSA approval means Samnel’s formulation and labelling have been reviewed against pharmaceutical-grade product standards — not the lighter requirements of ordinary food supplements. GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice) is an international framework covering raw material sourcing, sanitation, employee training, batch documentation, and recall procedures; every batch follows the same validated processes. FSSC 22000 (Food Safety System Certification) combines ISO 22000 with additional prerequisites for microbial control, allergen management, and supply-chain integrity.

SGS runs independent batch testing: identity (confirming actives are what the label says), potency (quantities match claims), heavy metals (lead, cadmium, mercury, arsenic), and microbial load. A certificate of analysis is available for any batch on request. When you take a Samnel softgel or Kids stick, you are not receiving a label claim — you are receiving a product that has passed four independent quality systems.

What HSA approval actually means

Singapore’s Health Sciences Authority (HSA) regulates therapeutic products, cosmetics, and health supplements under some of the most rigorous frameworks in Asia. For Samnel, HSA approval was not a single stamp — it was an iterative review of ingredient evidence, dose justification, claim substantiation, label content, and manufacturing compliance.

HSA reviewed Samnel’s formulation, dosing rationale, and labelling for both Samnel Adult (Calcifediol 20 mcg + Vitamin K₂ MK-7 90 mcg) and Samnel Kids (Calcifediol 10 mcg + calcium, magnesium, zinc). Health benefits asserted on the product labels are HSA-recognised. The trademark is registered in Singapore (as well as in the United States and China).

FSSC 22000 and GMP: how every batch is made

FSSC 22000 is a globally recognised food safety management system certification that combines ISO 22000 with specific prerequisite programmes and additional HACCP-based requirements. For a supplement manufacturer, FSSC 22000 means documented, audited processes for allergen control, hygiene zoning, pest management, supplier qualification, traceability, and recall readiness.

GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice) is the parallel framework focused on raw material identity, sanitation, personnel training, process validation, in-process quality checks, batch documentation, and finished-product release. Every Samnel batch is released only against documented evidence that identity, potency, and purity specifications have been met.

Our manufacturing partner, Kingmao International, is based in Singapore, holds FSSC 22000 and multiple GMP certifications, and is under ongoing oversight from the US FDA, the Singapore Food Agency (SFA), and HSA. Facility audits are routine, not exceptional.

SGS: the independent batch testing

SGS is one of the world’s largest independent testing and certification companies. Samnel uses SGS Singapore for each finished-batch quality panel. Every lot is tested for:

Identity: confirmation that the Calcifediol and K₂ actives are what the label says they are, by HPLC against reference standards.

Potency: quantitative measurement that the dose per unit meets label claim with the recognised tolerance band for supplement actives.

Heavy metals: lead, cadmium, mercury, and arsenic against international limits.

Microbial safety: total plate count, coliforms, yeasts and moulds, and absence of specified pathogens.

Samnel has also passed SGS testing required for the Singapore Customs Certificate of Free Sale, a document that confirms the product is legally sold in Singapore and is eligible for export.

Why four frameworks, not one

Any one of these frameworks, in isolation, would be reasonable. Together, they create overlapping, independent layers of accountability: a regulatory framework (HSA) reviewing formulation and claims; a facility framework (GMP) controlling manufacturing process integrity; a system framework (FSSC 22000) controlling food-safety management; and a testing framework (SGS) verifying that the output meets specification every batch.

The practical consequence: when you take a Samnel softgel or Kids stick, you are not trusting a brand promise. You are relying on four separate audited systems, any one of which can flag a problem and stop a batch from reaching you.
